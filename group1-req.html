Proof of date of birth

  • DOB is verified against the SSLC (10th) or HSC (12th) mark sheet issued by the Tamil Nadu Directorate of Government/School Examinations.

Educational Qualification கல்வித் தகுதி

  • Generally a Degree from a University established under the Tamil Nadu Universities Act / UGC-recognised university, in subjects relevant to the post. Post 1003 (DSP) requires additionally B.L./B.Com or equivalent combinations as specified.
  • Qualification must be obtained on or before the notification date (23.06.2026). If awaiting final results, a provisional degree/provisional certificate with consolidated mark sheet (per Annexure V format) is acceptable, to be uploaded by a later prescribed date.
  • MBBS candidates must have completed internship; specific conditions apply — see §4.2.3.4 of notification.
  • Candidates with a higher qualification than required must also upload proof of the prescribed minimum qualification.
  • Equivalence claims for non-standard degrees must be backed by an order from the relevant authority, uploaded at the time of certificate verification — failing which the claim is rejected after due process.
  • If the original degree certificate is delayed, an extract from the degree register/provisional record is accepted as proof.
Always verify equivalence orders against the Tamil Nadu State Council for Higher Education portal: www.tnsche.tn.gov.in

Medical & Physical Fitness மருத்துவ & உடற்தகுதி

  • Selected candidates must produce a Medical Fitness Certificate to the appointing authority at the time of posting (not at application stage).
  • For DSP (Post 1003) physical/medical standards (height, chest, vision via eye specialist, etc.) apply as per police service rules.
  • Eyesight certification by a qualified Eye Specialist required where applicable, particularly for police-related posts.

Tamil Language Eligibility தமிழ் மொழித் தகுதி

  • Candidates must possess adequate knowledge of Tamil as on the date of notification.
  • Satisfied by: passing Tamil as one language subject at SSLC/equivalent or degree level; OR having Tamil as medium of instruction at SSLC/equivalent; OR passing TNPSC's SLC (Second Class Language Test — full test) in Tamil.
  • If proof of Tamil proficiency cannot be furnished at application time, the candidate must pass the SLC Tamil test within 2 years of joining service, failing which they will be removed from service.

Restrictions on Applying விண்ணப்பிப்பதில் கட்டுப்பாடுகள்

Candidates (other than SC, SC(A), ST, MBC/DNC, BC(M), BC) who have already completed 5 years or more of regular service in Central/State Government from their date of first joining are not eligible to apply for these posts, regardless of being within the age limit.

Selection Process — Overview தேர்வு செயல்முறை

  1. Preliminary Examination (OMR, objective type) — qualifying stage; merit list used only to shortlist for the Main exam (ratio of roughly 1:20 per the notification).
  2. Main Examination — descriptive, written, comprising 4 papers (General Studies I–IV) plus a Tamil/English qualifying-type component as specified.
  3. Certificate Verification for those provisionally shortlisted based on Main exam marks.
  4. Interview / Viva-voce for posts that carry one (per Annexure provisions), worth up to 100 marks combined with personality test where applicable.
  5. Final Selection — based on Main exam marks + interview marks (where applicable), subject to medical fitness and document verification.
Marks scored in the Preliminary exam are not added to the final merit — Prelim is purely a screening/qualifying stage (§5.7–5.11 in source). Final merit = Main exam marks (+ interview marks where applicable).

Tie-break rules for merit list (§5.11)

  1. Candidates with equal total marks: the older candidate (by date of birth) ranks higher.
  2. If age is also equal: candidate with higher Main exam marks ranks higher.
  3. If Main exam marks also equal: candidate who submitted the online application earlier ranks higher.

How to Apply விண்ணப்பிப்பது எப்படி

Step 1 — One-Time Registration (OTR)

  • Register at www.tnpscexams.in in the OTR section; pay ₹150 registration fee. OTR is valid for 5 years.
  • OTR is not an application for any specific post — it only creates your personal profile. A separate online application is still required for each exam.
  • A working personal email ID and mobile number are mandatory and must be kept active continuously. Never share your password/OTP/email credentials with anyone.
  • Linking Aadhaar to your OTR is mandatory (used only for identity verification via CIDR; never stored or shared otherwise).
  • Enter SSLC details (register no., certificate no., year/month of passing, medium, board) accurately — errors can lead to summary rejection of the application later.

Step 2 — Online Application for this exam

  • Login with your OTR User ID & Password, click "APPLY" against this notification.
  • Review pre-filled OTR data; edit via "OTR Edit" if anything is wrong (changes apply to applications submitted thereafter).
  • Upload a fresh photograph taken after the notification date (see photo specs below) for this specific application.
  • Select up to two exam centre preferences (PWD candidates select only one); select only one venue within the chosen district/zone.
  • Use "SAVE AND PROCEED" as you complete each section; use "PREVIEW" to receive a preview copy by email/SMS before final submission.
  • Click "SUBMIT" to finally lodge the application — only after this is the application considered filed. A mere preview email does not count as a submitted application.
  • Pay the requisite fee online to complete the process.

Step 3 — Correction Window

Open from 02.08.2026 to 04.08.2026. Use this window to correct any errors in the submitted application. No changes are possible after this window closes. Only the latest, finally-submitted data is considered for processing.

Applying close to the deadline risks server overload / payment failures. TNPSC strongly advises applying well in advance of the last date rather than waiting until 29.07.2026.

Photograph & Signature Specifications புகைப்படம் & கையொப்பம்

Photograph

  • Recent (within 3 months), colour, passport-size: 4.5 cm (height) × 3.5 cm (width) — 170 × 130 pixels.
  • White background; face, both ears and neck clearly visible; correct studio-quality format.
  • Name and date of photo capture printed below the photo.
  • Face occupies 3.0 cm of the 4.5 cm height; name/date strip occupies 1.5 cm.
  • File: "Photograph.jpg", 20KB–50KB, 200 DPI.
  • Not accepted: selfies, mobile-camera shots, photocopies/Xerox, group/family photos, travel photos, photos with trees/flags/buildings as background.

Signature

  • Sign within a 6.0 cm × 2.0 cm box (230 × 75 pixels) drawn on plain white paper, using blue or black ink pen.
  • Scan at 200 DPI; file: "Signature.jpg", 10KB–20KB.
A fresh photo taken on/after the notification date (or earlier) must be uploaded freshly for every application — re-using an old uploaded photo from the OTR alone is not sufficient unless it meets all current criteria.

Document Upload Rules ஆவணங்கள் பதிவேற்றம்

  • Candidates shortlisted for certificate verification must upload supporting documents for everything claimed in the online application, when called for.
  • Documents uploaded get linked to the candidate's OTR and stored on the server for a maximum of 2 years. Within that window, the same documents need not be re-uploaded for future applications — they'll auto-display for confirmation.
  • After 2 years from upload, fresh document upload is required for any new application.
  • Any discrepancy between data entered online and documents submitted leads to rejection after due process.

Category-wise Concessions & Reservation இட ஒதுக்கீடு & சலுகைகள்

Ex-Servicemen No fee for 2 attempts

Defined per Annexure II §1 (specific release-date and service conditions apply). Fee exemption limited to two attempts. Reservation in vacancies per government order. Documents required: Bonafide/Discharge Certificate from Ex-servicemen welfare directorate, Pension Pay Order, prescribed Self-Declaration form, and (if still due for release within a year of last application date) a Commanding Officer certificate.

Persons with Benchmark Disabilities Fee exempt, 1 centre only

40%+ specified disability as defined under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, certified via Forms V/VI/VII/VIII by notified medical authorities (see full forms reproduced in Annexure II). 1% additional reservation within general category allocations (split across categories a–c at 1% and d–e at 1%).

Destitute Widows Fee exempt

Total monthly income from all sources (including family pension/self-employment) not exceeding ₹8,000. Must be a widow as on notification date. Certificate required from Revenue Divisional Officer / Tahsildar / Sub-Collector in the prescribed format (Annexure II §3.4).

Tamil Medium Educated Preference benefit

Candidates who studied SSLC, HSC and Degree entirely in Tamil medium are eligible for preferential treatment under the PSTM (Amendment) Act 2020. Prescribed school/institution certificates required for every institution attended, including special formats for changed school names or direct RTE Act admissions.

SC / SC(Arunthathiyar) / ST 15% / 3% / 1% reservation

SC: 15% · SC(A): 3% · ST: 1% reservation. Caste certificate must be obtained from the Tahsildar (SC/SC(A)) or Revenue Divisional Officer/Sub-Collector/equivalent (ST) of the candidate's permanent residence, citing father's/mother's name. Certificates not naming the candidate's father/mother are rejected.

Backward Classes (BC / BC-Muslim / MBC&DNC) 26.5% / 3.5% / 20% reservation

Non-Muslim BC: 26.5% · BC(Muslim): 3.5% · MBC & Denotified Communities: 20%. Caste certificate from Tahsildar / Deputy Tahsildar / RDO / authorised officer as listed in §6.5.1 of Annexure II, citing parent's name. Conversion to a religion outside Hinduism (except Islam, for BC-M) moves the candidate to "Others" category.

Women Minimum 30% horizontal reservation

Minimum 30% of vacancies in every category (and overall) reserved for women candidates, who may also compete in the remaining 70% open to all genders.

Transgender Persons Identity card via TN Transgender Welfare Board

Identity verified via card issued by the Tamil Nadu Transgender Welfare Board only (cards from other bodies not accepted). Transgender candidates without a caste certificate may opt for "Others" or "MBC" category in their application; those with an SC/SC(A)/ST caste certificate are treated per that community.

Exam Day Rules & Banned Items தேர்வு நாள் விதிகள்

Banned items inside the exam hall

Mobile phones, pagers, programmable devices/calculators, smart watches, pen drives, any storage media, memory notes/chits, Bluetooth devices, communication chips, P&G Design Data Books, math/drawing instruments, log/slide rules, printed notes, loose/rough sheets, and bags of any kind are strictly prohibited inside the exam hall.
  • Candidates found with banned items will not be permitted to write the exam; their answer sheet(s) will be invalidated and the application rejected after due process. They may also be debarred from future exams and face departmental/criminal action if cheating is suspected.
  • TNPSC accepts no responsibility for personal items (e.g., mobile phones) candidates bring at their own risk.

Reporting & gate timing

  • Arrive at least 1 hour before the scheduled start time.
  • All entry gates close 30 minutes before the exam start — no entry after that, under any circumstance.
  • Bring the downloaded Hall Ticket plus one original photo ID (Aadhaar / Passport / Driving Licence / PAN / Voter ID).
  • Frisking by hall invigilators/authorised staff may be conducted as needed. Parents/companions are not allowed inside the exam venue.
  • Sit only in your allotted seat at your allotted centre — seat or centre changes are not permitted.
  • No food, tea, coffee, snacks or beverages allowed inside the exam hall.
  • Use only black ballpoint/gel/fountain pen for OMR marking.

OMR sheet rules (Preliminary exam)

  • Darken exactly one circle per question using black ink ballpoint pen only — multiple markings void that question's evaluation.
  • Write and darken the Question Booklet Number exactly as printed on your booklet — incorrect entry invalidates the OMR sheet.
  • Sign in the designated space — an unsigned OMR sheet is invalidated.
  • Affix left-hand thumb impression — 0.5 mark is deducted from total score if omitted.
  • Mark "Yes"/"No" correctly for whether all 200 questions were attempted, and write the count of un-attempted questions if "No".

Scribe / Writer facility

Available for visually impaired and locomotor-disabled candidates meeting the prescribed criteria, per Tamil Nadu Government Servants (Conditions of Service) Act 2016 §2(s)/2(r) definitions. Compensatory time and/or a TNPSC-appointed scribe may be allotted on request with the prescribed certificate (Annexure V Part A). No separate payment is required for using a TNPSC-arranged scribe.

Answer Key, Interview & Final Stages விடைத்தாள் சாவி & இறுதிக் கட்டங்கள்

  • Provisional Answer Key: published on the TNPSC website within 6 working days of the Preliminary exam. Challenges can be filed online via the "Answer Key Challenge" portal within 7 days of publication — no postal/email challenges accepted.
  • Challenges are reviewed by a subject-expert committee; the final answer key is decided based on their recommendation, after which OMR evaluation proceeds. Final answer keys are not published until the entire selection process concludes.
  • Certificate Verification: shortlisted candidates must bring all original certificates uploaded/re-uploaded online. No re-scheduling is offered for missed verification dates — failing to attend forfeits the interview stage.
  • Interview/Personality Test: conducted on the date/time/venue notified; no rescheduling for missed dates.
  • Medical Examination: at the notified date, time, and location for finally selected candidates; missing it forfeits inclusion in the final merit list.
  • Final Merit List: candidates are ranked strictly by merit; post allotment, once made during counselling, cannot be changed under any circumstances.
